# Domain Model

## 1. Purpose

The Domain Model defines the core business concepts of the Enterprise Asset Management Platform (EAMP). It provides a technology-independent view of the business entities, their responsibilities, and their relationships.

This document is the bridge between business requirements and solution architecture.

---

# 2. Domain Overview

The platform manages the complete lifecycle of organizational assets.

```text
Asset Lifecycle

Register
    ↓
Assign
    ↓
Use
    ↓
Transfer
    ↓
Maintain
    ↓
Return
    ↓
Dispose
```

---

# 3. Core Domain

The core domain of the platform is **Asset Lifecycle Management**.

Everything else supports this domain.

Supporting domains include:

* Identity & Access
* Workflow
* Notifications
* Reporting
* Audit & Compliance

---

# 4. Domain Entities

## User

Represents a person who interacts with the platform.

Responsibilities

* Authenticate
* Perform business actions
* Receive notifications
* Own approvals

---

## Role

Represents business permissions.

Examples

* Administrator
* Asset Manager
* Department Manager
* Technician
* Employee
* Auditor

---

## Permission

Represents a specific capability.

Examples

* Create Asset
* Update Asset
* Delete Asset
* Approve Assignment
* Manage Users

---

## Asset

Represents any physical asset owned by the organization.

Examples

* Laptop
* Desktop
* Vehicle
* Server
* Printer
* Medical Device
* Machinery

---

## Asset Category

Logical grouping of assets.

Examples

* IT Equipment
* Furniture
* Vehicle
* Medical Equipment

---

## Location

Physical location of an asset.

Examples

* HQ
* Warehouse
* Branch Office

---

## Assignment

Represents the allocation of an asset.

Assignment may target:

* User
* Department
* Location

---

## Maintenance Request

Represents maintenance work.

Lifecycle

Open

↓

Assigned

↓

In Progress

↓

Completed

---

## Approval

Represents approval workflow.

Examples

* Assignment Approval
* Transfer Approval
* Disposal Approval

---

## Notification

Represents communication generated by business events.

---

## Audit Log

Represents immutable business history.

# 10. Domain Rules

Examples

* Asset Number must be unique.
* Asset must have one active status.
* Asset can have only one active assignment.
* Returned assets cannot remain assigned.
* Disposed assets cannot be reassigned.
* Audit records are immutable.
